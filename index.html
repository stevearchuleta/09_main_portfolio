PRACTICE
07 April 2011


Practice silence, practice love;
Practice genteelness, rise above
Your doubts and fears, your greed and pain,
Practice patience during midst of blame.


Enchant your practice with hope and wonder,
Practice stillness while crawling under
The judgements, bashings, and societal norms,
Practice peace while planting corms.


Let wind, and cloud, and soil, and light
Fulfill your lack of practice right;
Then practice peace like aqueous flow
Least resistance, until you know


That practice for you is different from me,
Practice builds roots to steady our tree.
Your clutter, your crime, our dollar, our dime
Disorients the practice inside you Devine.


So practice stopping, practice thought,
Practice acceptance, if even bought;
Yet pay your mind and physical realm
A debt of practice, to steer your helm!


Practice, practice,
Makes perfect or not;
The nothingness of practice
Means quite a lot.


Steven Archuleta

PANGS OF CELEBRATION
20 July 2017

Inaudible vibrations said goodbye
As her sorrows set within;
Years of never knowing
Her last or next of kin.


Pages in that diary book
She wrote upon each day,
Contemplative her shaking pen
With nothing much to say.


'Til joy rang out in silent ink,
Her songs from trough to crest,
The waves so high within her soul
Her writing greets her best-
Friend's love between their
Childhood play and adolescent studies,
From city taverns to sandy shores
To official fuddy duddies.


They married men and bore each child,
They cooked and baked and laughed,
Played cards 'til twelve
And through book-clubs shelved
Perspectives - not quartered but halved.


Yet today she lay so empty and still
Her corpse without her spirit,
A BFF no longer present
To assist her when she fear it:


Those inaudible vibrations,
That nothingness - isolation...
Becomes us all,
Death's boisterous call,
In pangs of celebration.


Steven Archuleta

Luigi | 25July 2012

CELLULOSE (by Polly Papyrus)
19 July 2013


Tenderly you grasp me by my edge,
No margins yet defined, my fibrous pledge:
To hold your heart and soul,
It’s all so notable,
A brilliant tool of love, a pitching wedge.


Isotropic, intertwined, air-laid are we,
Said carefully, “produced from that-there tree.”
Our fat to thin design,
Kroyer’s assembly line,
Hammermill, or better, hammer me!


Our parables and understated rhymes,
Our hopes and fears and censored joy-filled times,
No laughter written down,
Evoked without a sound,
Please scribe upon my nakedness ten times.


Eight point five, eleven. legal quickie,
A4 us, ream by ream, perhaps a sticky,
My glycerin is sweet,
A tasty recycled treat,
Caliper, opacity, not dicky!


Industrial, yet edible,
Ethnically amenable,
A tearful pulp my sheets get ever stained,
Your tube of ink and tip,
No tortioned wire clip,
Just fill and flow ’til fully entertained.


Yes! Grainy roll-play tales: fictional raper!
Bond, though moist, and permeable to vapor.
Outside your whited space,
Inside my fall from grace,
Impregnated by nothing more than paper.


Steven Archuleta
written on 19 July 2013
